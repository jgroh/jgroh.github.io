About

I'm an evolutionary geneticist interested in the origin and maintenance of variation in genes, traits, and species. My current research focuses on the evolution, ecology, and molecular biology of mating systems as a window into convergent adaptation, the origin of novelty, and the genetic architecture of ecologically significant traits. I am particularly interested in flowering plants as a model for understanding the resolution of sexual conflict in hermaphrodites. Other research interests include population divergence, consequences of hybridization, and temporal niche specialization. I'm currently a Miller Postdoctoral Fellow at UC Berkeley working with Noah Whiteman and Ben Blackman. Prior to this I completed my PhD in Population Biology at UC Davis with Graham Coop.
